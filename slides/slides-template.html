The Standard Deviation

Another measure of variation, the standard deviation describes the mean distance between the data values and the mean of the dataset.

Key Facts about the Standard Deviation

  • The standard deviation is always non-negative. (\(s\geq 0\))
  • The larger the standard deviation, the more variation is in the data.
  • If the standard deviation is equal to zero, there is no variation in the data.

The Standard Deviation

Another measure of variation, the standard deviation describes the mean distance between the data values and the mean of the dataset.

Key Facts about the Standard Deviation

  • The standard deviation is always non-negative. (\(s\geq 0\))
  • The larger the standard deviation, the more variation is in the data.
  • If the standard deviation is equal to zero, there is no variation in the data.

Standard deviation formula

\[s=\sqrt{\frac{\Sigma(x-\bar{x})^2}{n-1}}\]

Remember, \(\bar{x}\) is the sample mean and \(n\) is the sample size.

I'll Do An Example:

We will calculate the standard deviation of a small dataset to understand how the formula works.
Consider the dataset below.
\(\begin{array}{ccccccc} 1&2&3&3&4&6&9 \end{array}\)

What is the mean of this data?

\[\bar{x}=4\]

Next, we will find the difference between each data value and the mean.

\(x\) \(x-\bar{x}\)
\(1\) \((1-4)=-3\)
\(2\) \((2-4)=-2\)
\(3\) \((3-4)=-1\)
\(3\) \((3-4)=-1\)
\(4\) \((4-4)=0\)
\(6\) \((6-4)=2\)
\(9\) \((9-4)=5\)

NEXT

\[x\] \[x-\bar{x}\] \[(x-\bar{x})^2\]
\(1\) \(1-4=-3\) \((-3)^2=9\)
\(2\) \(2-4=-2\) \((-2)^2=4\)
\(3\) \(3-4=-1\) \((-1)^2=1\)
\(3\) \(3-4=-1\) \((-1)^2=1\)
\(4\) \(4-4=0\) \((0)^2=0\)
\(6\) \(6-4=2\) \((2)^2=4\)
\(9\) \(9-4=5\) \((5)^2=25\)
Sum (or add together) the resulting squares. \[9+4+1+1+0+4+25=44 \]

Divide the sum.

\[ \frac{44}{7-1} = \frac{44}{7}=7.333 \]

Finally, take the square root.

\[ \sqrt{7.333} \approx 2.71\]
